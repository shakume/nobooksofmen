No Books of Men

A Place in the Pack

The loose structure of shifters are as follows:

  1. The Land: The only thing more important than the individual's freedom is one's duty to the land itself. All Shifters are tied to the land of their origins and share a kinship with the animals native to that region. They can travel abroad if they wish, but the longer they roam away from home the more antsy and uncomfortable they become.
  2. The Individual: Ultimately, the Shifters walk alone. They know and work with others when needed, but most are loners to one degree or another and prefer to work alone or with a small group of 2-3 others (see below). Even when they choose to associate with a pack (or Grand Pack), their own freedom always comes first.
  3. The Pack: When Shifters befriend and bond with a group of other shifters (or less often other Mortals or Supernaturals) they claim themselves to be a part of a "Pack." The Pack are those who you would risk your health and life for if need be, with the expectation they would do the same for you.
  4. The Grand Pack: A loose, regional confederation of Packs in a given collection of territories is called a Grand Pack. The Grand Pack meets rarely and has no real power beyond those that its constituent packs agree to grant it. The Grand Pack's sole purpose is to ensure the overall health of a region's nature and wildlife.
  5. The Conspiracy: Not technically part of the "hierarchy" structure, but if present the Conspiracy to which a Shifter belongs is wholly secondary to the obligations of the Land, the Self, the Pack, and the Grand Pack. These allegiances are outside the natural order, and thus are an afterthought even for those who choose to associate with them.
